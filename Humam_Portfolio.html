HR Analysis

Salary Prediction and HR Analytics for Data Professionals - developed a robust salary prediction model and conducted in-depth HR analytics to identify key drivers of salary, assess pay equity, and provide data-driven insights for compensation strategies.

Project Description

This project focuses on developing a robust salary prediction model and conducting in-depth HR analytics for data professionals within a company. Leveraging a comprehensive dataset that includes demographic information, employment details, performance ratings, and past experience, the project aims to identify key drivers of salary, assess pay equity, and provide data-driven insights for compensation strategies. The ultimate goal is to create a fair and transparent compensation framework that aligns with market trends and internal performance.

The analytical process began with meticulous data cleaning and preprocessing. The initial dataset contained various attributes such as FIRST NAME, LAST NAME, SEX, DOJ (Date of Joining), CURRENT DATE, DESIGNATION, AGE, SALARY, UNIT, LEAVES USED, LEAVES REMAINING, RATINGS, and PAST EXP. Irrelevant columns were dropped, duplicates removed, and null values handled.

Key findings included identifying AGE, PAST EXP, DESIGNATION, UNIT, and RATINGS as key salary drivers. The optimized ElasticNet model achieved exceptional performance on the test set, with a Mean Absolute Error (MAE) of approximately $4,600 and an R-squared value exceeding 0.90.

Car Market Analysis

Comprehensive analysis of car sales data to uncover insights into sales performance, pricing strategies, and market segmentation.

Project Description

This project delves into a comprehensive analysis of car sales data, aiming to uncover critical insights into sales performance, pricing strategies, and market segmentation. By examining a dataset containing various attributes such as Manufacturer, Model, Fuel type, Engine size, Mileage, Year of manufacture, and Price, the analysis provides a detailed understanding of the factors influencing car sales and pricing dynamics.

Key findings revealed that Ford, VW, and Toyota emerged as top manufacturers in terms of sales volume. The analysis confirmed that a car's age and mileage are the most significant factors determining its price, followed by engine size. A counter-intuitive finding was that the average Toyota RAV4 (Compact SUV) was priced higher than the BMW X3, suggesting that age is a dominant pricing factor over brand prestige in this specific instance.

The project successfully identified key drivers of sales volume, revenue, and pricing, while also shedding light on market segmentation and customer preferences. The findings underscore the importance of factors such as age, mileage, engine size, and fuel type in determining car prices.

Adult Income Analysis

Income Prediction and Socio-Economic Factor Analysis - comprehensive EDA to investigate factors influencing income levels within a demographic dataset.

Project Description

This project undertakes a comprehensive Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) to investigate the factors influencing income levels within a demographic dataset. The primary objective was to uncover significant socio-economic indicators that correlate with an individual's income, specifically distinguishing between those earning <=50K and >50K annually.

The analysis revealed a significant imbalance in income distribution, with approximately 76% of individuals earning <=50K and 24% earning >50K. Education emerged as a clear and significant determinant of income, with a correlation coefficient of 0.33. The analysis highlighted a non-linear relationship, where obtaining a Bachelor's degree serves as a critical inflection point, dramatically increasing an individual's chances of earning over $50K.

The project uncovered significant income disparities based on sex and race, which persisted even after controlling for education and occupation. For instance, among Bachelor's degree holders, 49.5% of men earned >50K, compared to only 22.9% of women. Occupation was identified as a crucial factor in determining the return on education.
